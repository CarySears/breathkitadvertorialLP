A simple reset you can do anywhere

A small shift can create a big result. When you slow the exhale, you help "turn on" the parasympathetic nervous system (rest and digest) and tone down the sympathetic system (fight or flight). Research on breath focus and diaphragmatic breathing links this style of practice with the relaxation response and calmer nervous system activity.

Your breath is always with you, but most of us were never taught how to use it. When stress spikes, breathing tends to become fast and shallow without us noticing. By changing the breath, you can change the message your body receives, and that can shift mood, attention, and emotional balance in real time.

  1. Place one hand on your chest and one on your belly.
  2. Inhale gently through the nose for a count of 4.
  3. Exhale slowly for a count of 6 to 8.
  4. Repeat for six to eight rounds or about two minutes.

If you feel lightheaded, shorten the count or stop. You can always start with a smaller range like 3 in, 4 out.
